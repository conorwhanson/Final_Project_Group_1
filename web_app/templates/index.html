Why do employees leave?

The disruption of employee attrition is felt not only by the employer,
but also by the employee. Uncovering the underlying reasons employees leave
is crucial to preventing employee burnout, disruption to business operations,
and tarnished business reputations. By deploying a variety of data science and
data analysis tools, this project seeks to offer data-driven insights into this question.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics in a 2020 Employee Tenure Summary:

Median job tenure was 4.1 years,
a slight decrease from 4.2 in 2018.

Only 29 % of men and 27 % of women (as wage/salary workers) had 10+ years with their current employer.

The median tenure for wage and salary workers in the public sector was 6.5 years, while the private sector was half of that at 3.7 years.

Among different occupations Management and professional workers had the highest median tenure (4.9 years), while workers in the food service industry had the lowest median tenure (1.9 years).
